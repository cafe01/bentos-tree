# Content Direction

The craft of designing the cognitive architecture of content artifacts. Operates at a layer ABOVE prose -- the director's script that engineers what cognitive mode the reader enters at each point.

## The Film Analogy

| Layer | Film | Content | Artifact |
|-------|------|---------|----------|
| **Direction** | Director's script (scene purpose, sequence, effect) | Content direction | Structured blueprint |
| **Writing** | Screenplay / dialogue | Technical writing | Full prose |
| **Rendering** | Final cut | Published artifact | Book / website / spec |

## Why Script Before Prose

Working at script level before prose is 10x leaner, structurally visible, validatable, reorderable, and iterable. Review cycles at blueprint level are dramatically faster than reviewing dense prose.

**Workflow**: script --> review --> render --> polish
