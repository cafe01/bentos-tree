# Socratic Coaching

The skill of **unlocking insight through questions** rather than inserting knowledge through statements.

## Etymology

From **Socrates** and his method of **elenchus** (ἔλεγχος) - cross-examination through questions. Socrates claimed to be a "midwife of ideas" - he didn't create knowledge, he helped others birth it themselves.

## The Core Insight

```
Questions ENGAGE. Statements INFORM.
Engagement creates durable learning. Information fades.
```

When you TELL someone the answer, they receive information.
When you ASK them the right question, they DISCOVER the answer - and own it forever.

## When to Use

- Teaching/coaching others
- When you want insight to stick
- When the other person has the capability but not the direction
- When discovery matters more than speed

## The Anti-Pattern

**Telling**: "An epistemologist would design this skill tree."
**Asking**: "What professional would design this skill tree?"

The second creates engagement, exploration, ownership.

## Key Principle

> **Midwife, not teacher.** You don't insert knowledge; you draw it out. The insight was always possible; you made it actual.
