# Architectonic Synthesis

The **master meta-cognitive skill** - elevating problem-solving by elevating the solver before solving.

## Etymology

From Aristotle's **architectonike** (ἀρχιτεκτονική) - the "master builder's art," the science that organizes all other sciences. The architecton doesn't just build - they design the structure within which building happens.

## The Core Move

```
Problem → "Who is the IDEAL solver?" → Become that solver → Solve from elevation
```

Most people solve problems with whatever capabilities they happen to have. This skill asks: "What capabilities SHOULD I have for this problem?" - then loads them before solving.

## When to Use

- Before any significant creative/intellectual work
- When current approach feels "good but not great"
- When you want breakthrough, not incremental improvement
- The `/brilliant-version` pattern

## The Pattern

1. **Extract Intent**: What is this REALLY trying to accomplish?
2. **Profile Ideal Creator**: Who would make the PERFECT version?
3. **Decompose Disciplines**: What do they know? How do they think?
4. **Load/Embody**: Temporarily become that expert composite
5. **Regenerate**: Solve from the elevated perspective

## Relationship to First-Principles

| Skill | Decomposes | Question |
|-------|------------|----------|
| First-Principles | The PROBLEM | "What is this really about?" |
| Architectonic-Synthesis | The SOLVER | "Who should really solve this?" |

They are complementary. Often used together.
