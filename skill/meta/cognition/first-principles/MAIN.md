# First-Principles Thinking

The cognitive skill of decomposing problems to their fundamental truths before attempting solutions.

## When to Use

- When inherited solutions feel inadequate
- When "best practices" aren't working
- When you need breakthrough, not incremental improvement
- Before any architectonic synthesis

## The Core Question

> "What is this REALLY about?"

Keep asking until you hit bedrock - physics, logic, mathematics, or irreducible human nature.

## Etymology

From Aristotle's *arche* (ἀρχή) - the first principle from which a thing can be known.
